Detailed Portfolios (Article V)

Chairperson

Presides over meetings, oversees all activities, and holds the power of appointment for sub-committees.

Vice Chairperson

Assesses and reports on member welfare, academic stability, and social health. Receives all meeting apologies.

Speaker

Interprets the Constitution and ensures that every voice is respected during discussions.

Disciplinarian

Vets all new applicants, oversees induction, and maintains discipline during all gatherings.

Article IX: The House of Laws

Rigid rules of conduct for all members:

  • Punctuality: Late arrival after 3:00 p.m. attracts a fine of KSh 20.
  • Digital Decorum: All phones shall remain on silent mode during meetings.
  • Financial Punctuality: Contribution of KSh 10 per meeting to the Summit account.
  • Attendance: Missing 2 consecutive meetings without apology attracts a fine of KSh 50.
  • Leadership Accountability: Leaders missing without apology are fined KSh 100.
  • Succession: A leader missing 3 consecutive meetings shall lose their position.
  • Apologies: Must be submitted before 2:30 p.m. to the Vice Chairperson.

Core Strategic Objectives

Roadmap to translate Growth, Opportunity, and Impact:

  • Capacity Building: Knowledge, moral values, and financial literacy.
  • Professional Synergy: Community of high-achieving individuals.
  • Leadership Democratization: A "Home for All" where anyone can rise.
  • Public Impact: Outreach for the less fortunate and responsible living.

Constitutional Preamble

"We, the members of the YOUTH EMPOWERMENT SUMMIT (YES), united by a shared purpose of nurturing responsible, informed, and capable young people, hereby establish this Constitution... we pledge to uphold this document as the supreme guiding framework."

Mission

Ensuring the continuous development and overall well-being of every individual member, engaging them in acts of service to promote collective well-being as well as equipping them with necessary skills to become visionary leaders.
